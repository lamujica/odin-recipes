Llapingachos

Llapingachos is a traditional Ecuadorian dish, consisting of potato patties stuffed with cheese, then grilled to a golden crisp. It is often served with peanut sauce, sausage, fried eggs, and a tangy salad, creating a rich and comforting meal.This dish is considered one of the biggest representative icons of highland food.

Ingredients

  • 5 large russet potatoes
  • 2 tbs high smoke point oil
  • 1/2 cup minced white onions
  • 1 cup grated Queso Blanco
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes until soft
  2. On medium heat, saute onions and achiote until onions are translucent, this is known as a refrito, about 5 mins
  3. Mash potatoes and mix in with refrito. Add salt to taste
  4. Cover your potato dough and let it sit for about an hour in room temperature.
  5. Make small golf size balls with the potato dough
  6. Make a hole in the middle of each ball and fill with about 1 tbsp grated cheese
  7. Shape dough into thick patties, about 1 inch nd let rest in fridge for an hour
  8. Cook patties on a hot pan/griddle, until brown on each side. Careful not to break when flipping
  9. Serve with preferred sides
